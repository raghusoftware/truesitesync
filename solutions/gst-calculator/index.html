CGST, SGST & IGST — what's the difference

GST in India splits differently depending on where the buyer and seller are located. For a sale within the same state, the GST rate is split equally into CGST (Central GST, goes to the centre) and SGST (State GST, goes to the state) — each is exactly half the total rate. For a sale between two different states, the full rate is charged as a single IGST (Integrated GST), collected by the centre and apportioned to the destination state. The total tax paid is the same either way — only the split changes.

How the calculation works

Adding GST

GST amount = base amount × (rate ÷ 100). Total = base amount + GST amount.

Removing GST

Base amount = total ÷ (1 + rate ÷ 100). GST amount = total − base amount.

Splitting the tax

Intra-state: CGST = SGST = GST amount ÷ 2. Inter-state: IGST = full GST amount.

Frequently asked questions

How do you calculate GST on an amount?

To add: GST = base × (rate÷100), total = base + GST. To remove: base = total ÷ (1 + rate÷100), GST = total − base.

What is the difference between CGST, SGST and IGST?

Intra-state sales split the rate equally into CGST + SGST. Inter-state sales charge the full rate as IGST. Total tax is the same either way.

What is the current GST rate on cement?

18% (HSN 2523), reduced from 28% after the September 2025 rate reform.

What is the GST rate on steel/TMT bars?

18% across all diameters and grades (HSN 7213/7214).

What GST rate applies to a construction works contract?

18% generally; 12% with full ITC for government infrastructure works; 1% for affordable housing.

What are the current GST slabs in India?

Since September 2025: 0%, 5%, 18%, and 40% for select luxury/sin goods, with a few transitional categories still at 12%/28%.
