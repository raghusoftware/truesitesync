True Site Sync vs Powerplay: daily site logs or billing?

Powerplay is built around daily site management — logs, photos, tasks, labour. True Site Sync is built around the billing side — measurement, BOQ and RA bills. Here's how they actually differ.

See True Site Sync pricingTry free

The short answer

Powerplay is a well-known Indian construction site-management app — its public feature list centers on daily logs, site photos, task assignment, labour attendance and material movement, aimed at keeping a large site team coordinated. True Site Sync focuses specifically on turning site measurements into BOQ-linked RA bills — the part of the job that eats the most time for a billing engineer or a small contractor doing their own accounts. The two solve different halves of running a site, and the entry pricing reflects the different audience: Powerplay's published plans start around ₹70,000+/year; True Site Sync starts at ₹2,500/year.

What Powerplay is built for

Based on its public product pages, Powerplay's strengths are daily progress logs, photo documentation, task tracking across office and site teams, labour attendance, and material request/movement tracking — useful for a large site team that needs everyone coordinated day to day. It's a mature, widely used app in the Indian market with a large install base.

What True Site Sync is built for

True Site Sync starts where the measurement book usually lives: record Nos × L × B × H against BOQ items, generate the priced abstract, and raise the RA bill with previous/current/balance quantity, retention and GST applied automatically — plus inventory tied to the same BOQ consumption, labour wages, and a cash-flow view of what each site is actually earning. It's the billing-and-accounts side of the job, done digitally instead of in a measurement book and Excel.

True Site Sync vs Powerplay — frequently asked questions

What is the difference between True Site Sync and Powerplay?

Powerplay centers on daily site logs, photos and task/labour tracking. True Site Sync centers on measurement-to-RA-bill billing with BOQ and retention.

Is Powerplay expensive compared to True Site Sync?

Powerplay's public pricing starts around ₹70,000+/year; True Site Sync starts at ₹2,500/year, scaling to ₹50,000/year for up to 50 users.

Does Powerplay do RA billing?

Its public feature list centers on progress/labour/material tracking, not BOQ-driven RA billing — which is True Site Sync's focus.

Can I use both?

Yes — some teams run a site-ops app for field coordination alongside True Site Sync for billing.

Which is better for a small contractor?

For a solo engineer or small team whose bottleneck is manual RA billing, True Site Sync's lower price and billing-first design usually fit better.
