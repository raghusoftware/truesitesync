Work Order vs BOQ: the difference, explained

Two terms that get mixed up on every site. Here's what each one actually means, how they relate, and how to keep billing tied to both.

BOQ — the priced "what and how much"

A Bill of Quantities (BOQ) is the itemised, priced schedule of work: each item of work, its unit, the estimated quantity, and the agreed rate. It's the basis for measuring and valuing work as it's executed. The BOQ answers what is to be done and how much it's worth per unit.

Work order — the "go ahead, on these terms"

A work order (WO) is the contractual instruction that authorises the work. It carries the WO number and date, the total order value, GST treatment, retention, validity/expiry and conditions. The work order answers who authorised it and on what commercial terms. The BOQ usually sits under a work order.

How they work together

  • The work order sets the commercial frame (value, GST, retention, validity)
  • The BOQ lists the priced items executed under that order
  • Measurements record executed quantity against BOQ items
  • RA bills value that quantity and bill it under the work order
  • The balance quantity and remaining order value always reconcile

A project commonly has several work orders, each with its own BOQ group, rates, GST and retention — so they must be tracked separately to avoid over-billing or mixing contracts.

FAQs

What is the difference between a work order and a BOQ?

The BOQ is the priced item-and-quantity list; the work order is the instruction authorising it, with the WO number, value, GST, retention and terms.

Can one work order have multiple BOQs?

A project often has several work orders, each with its own BOQ/BOQ group and its own rates, GST and retention — track each separately.

How do you track billing against both?
