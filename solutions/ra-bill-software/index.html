What is an RA bill — and why it's so easy to get wrong

A Running Account (RA) bill is a progress bill raised at intervals through a construction project. Instead of billing the whole contract at the end, the contractor measures the work done up to a cut-off date, values it at BOQ rates, and bills the difference between the cumulative value now and what was already billed in earlier RA bills. Retention is held back, GST is added, and the client pays against that running account.

It sounds simple, but the arithmetic compounds. By the third or fourth RA bill you're juggling cumulative quantities, previous-bill quantities, item-wise rates, retention percentages, GST splits and deductions — usually across several spreadsheets that don't talk to each other. One mistyped cell and the bill is short-paid, queried, or rejected. Worse, errors hide: an over-measured quantity in RA-2 only surfaces when the client's QS reconciles at RA-5, and now you're issuing a negative bill and a difficult conversation.

RA bill software removes the arithmetic entirely. True Site Sync keeps a single live chain — measurement → abstract → RA bill — so every figure on the bill is calculated from the work actually recorded on site, and is traceable straight back to that record.

The core idea: you should never type a quantity into a bill. You measure once, on site, and the bill builds itself — cumulative, previous and current quantities included.

Five RA billing mistakes this software prevents

Most RA bill disputes trace back to the same handful of errors. Because True Site Sync derives every figure from the site record and the work order, each of these simply can't happen the way it does in a spreadsheet.

1. Double-billing a quantity

In Excel it's easy to forget that part of an item was already claimed in the previous RA bill, so you bill the full cumulative quantity again. The software always carries the previous-bill quantity forward and bills only the difference — the classic over-claim is gone.

2. Wrong or outdated rates

When rates live in a separate sheet, an old rate sneaks into a new bill. Here rates come straight from the work order's BOQ, so every item is valued at the agreed figure, every time.

3. Forgetting retention or applying it twice

Retention is set once on the work order and deducted automatically on every RA bill at the right percentage — never missed, never doubled.

4. GST mistakes

CGST/SGST vs IGST, and the right SAC, are configured per work order and applied consistently, so the tax on the bill matches what your accountant expects.

5. Un-traceable figures

When a client's QS questions a quantity, you can open the exact measurement entry behind it instead of defending a number you can't reconstruct. That single capability resolves most billing arguments before they escalate.
