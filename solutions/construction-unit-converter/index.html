Why these conversions come up on every site

India's land and property market runs on square feet, but drawings, IS codes and most engineering references use the metric SI system — meters, square meters, cubic meters. Every contractor, site engineer and billing clerk ends up converting between the two constantly: a client quotes a plot in square feet, the structural drawing gives spans in meters, and the BOQ needs quantities in cubic meters for RA billing. Keeping these conversions accurate matters because a small unit error compounds fast — a 2% area miscalculation on a large slab is real money.

Frequently asked questions

How do you convert square feet to square meters?

Multiply by 0.092903. Example: 1000 sq ft × 0.092903 = 92.9 sq m.

How do you convert feet to meters?

Multiply by 0.3048. Example: 10 ft × 0.3048 = 3.048 m.

How many cement bags per cubic meter of concrete?

For a common M20 mix (1:1.5:3), roughly 8 bags (50 kg each) per m³, using the standard dry-volume method. Use the calculator above for other ratios.

What is the formula for steel rebar weight?

Weight (kg/m) = D² ÷ 162, where D is the bar diameter in mm. Multiply by length in meters for total weight.

How many square feet in a square meter?

1 square metre ≈ 10.7639 square feet.
